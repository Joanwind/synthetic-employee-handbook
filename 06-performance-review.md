# Performance Review Policy

## Review Cycle
Performance reviews are conducted twice per year, typically mid-year and at year-end. New employees will also complete a probationary review within their first six months. Reviews are aligned with business goals and project cycles to ensure relevance to current work. Managers may conduct additional check-ins throughout the year to support ongoing performance management.

## Evaluation Criteria
Employees are assessed based on a combination of individual performance, technical competence, and contribution to team objectives. Key criteria include quality of work, delivery against agreed goals, collaboration, problem-solving ability, and adherence to company values. For technical roles, this may also include code quality, system design, and innovation. Expectations are clearly defined at the beginning of each review cycle.

## Feedback Process
The performance review process includes a self-assessment followed by a manager evaluation and a formal review discussion. Feedback is expected to be clear, evidence-based, and focused on both strengths and areas for improvement. Employees are encouraged to actively participate in the discussion and provide upward feedback where appropriate. Agreed outcomes and key points are documented and retained for future reference.

## Development Planning
Each review includes the creation or update of an individual development plan tailored to the employee’s role and career goals. This may include training, mentorship, stretch assignments, or skill development in areas such as new technologies or leadership. Managers and employees share responsibility for tracking progress against development goals. The company supports continuous learning and provides access to relevant resources to facilitate growth.
