# IT and Security

## Acceptable Use
Company systems, including laptops, networks, email, and software, must be used primarily for business purposes. Limited personal use is permitted where it does not interfere with work responsibilities or breach company policies. Employees must not install unauthorised software, access inappropriate content, or engage in activities that could compromise system performance or security. All usage may be monitored in line with applicable data protection laws.

## Access Control
Employees are responsible for maintaining the confidentiality of their login credentials at all times. Strong passwords must be used and updated regularly in accordance with company guidelines, and multi-factor authentication (MFA) must be enabled where required. Sharing of accounts or passwords is strictly prohibited. Access to systems and data is granted based on role and will be reviewed periodically or revoked when no longer required.

## Data Handling
Sensitive or confidential company data must be accessed, stored, and shared only through approved systems and in line with data protection requirements, including GDPR. Employees must not download, copy, or transfer data to personal devices or external platforms without authorisation. Data should only be retained for as long as necessary and disposed of securely when no longer required. Any suspected data breach must be reported immediately.

## Remote Access
Remote access to company systems must be conducted through secure, company-approved methods such as VPN and managed devices. Employees must ensure they are working on secure networks and avoid using public or unsecured Wi-Fi unless protected by approved security measures. Devices used for remote work must be locked when unattended and protected with up-to-date security software. Any loss or theft of equipment must be reported without delay.

## Violations
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment. Serious breaches, such as unauthorised access, data misuse, or security negligence, may also lead to legal action. The company reserves the right to suspend system access during investigations of suspected violations. All employees are expected to cooperate fully with any security or compliance reviews.
