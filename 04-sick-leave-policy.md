# Sick Leave Policy

## Eligibility
Employees are entitled to statutory sick leave in accordance with Irish legislation, currently providing up to 5 paid sick days per year, subject to meeting eligibility criteria. To qualify, employees must have completed at least 13 weeks of continuous service and provide a valid medical certificate. Payment is made at 70% of normal daily earnings, subject to the statutory daily cap. Any additional company sick pay beyond statutory entitlement will be outlined in the employee’s contract.

## Notification Procedure
Employees must notify their line manager as soon as possible on the first day of absence, ideally before their normal start time. Notification should be made via phone or an agreed communication channel, stating the reason for absence and expected return date if known. Employees are required to keep their manager informed of any changes to their expected return. Failure to follow the notification procedure may result in the absence being treated as unauthorised.

## Medical Certification
A medical certificate (fit note) from a registered medical practitioner is required for all statutory sick leave claims. The certificate must cover the full duration of the absence and be submitted promptly in line with payroll deadlines. For absences extending beyond two consecutive days, the company may require certification even where statutory leave is not being claimed. The company reserves the right to request additional medical evidence where appropriate.

## Extended Illness
Where an employee is absent due to illness for an extended period, regular contact must be maintained with their manager and HR. The company may request updated medical reports to assess fitness to return to work and any necessary workplace adjustments. A return-to-work meeting will be arranged following long-term absence to support reintegration. In cases where an employee is unable to return to work within a reasonable timeframe, the company will review the situation in line with employment law and may explore options such as adjusted duties or, where necessary, termination on medical grounds.
