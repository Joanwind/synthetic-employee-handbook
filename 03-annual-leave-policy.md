# Annual Leave Policy

## Entitlement
Full-time employees are entitled to a minimum of 20 days of paid annual leave per leave year, in line with Irish legislation. Part-time employees receive leave on a pro-rata basis, calculated according to hours worked. The leave year runs from 1 January to 31 December unless otherwise specified in the employment contract. Annual leave accrues throughout the year and must be taken in accordance with business needs.

## Public Holidays
Employees are entitled to public holiday benefits in accordance with Irish law, which currently includes 10 public holidays per year. Full-time employees will receive a paid day off on the public holiday or an alternative benefit, as applicable. Part-time employees qualify for public holiday entitlements based on their working hours, provided they meet eligibility criteria. The company will publish an annual list of recognised public holidays at the beginning of each year.

## Request Process
All annual leave must be requested in advance through the company’s leave management system or as otherwise directed by HR. Employees are expected to provide reasonable notice, typically at least two weeks for short leave and longer for extended periods. Requests should include clear dates and any relevant handover arrangements. Employees should not make travel commitments until leave has been formally approved.

## Approval
Annual leave requests are subject to approval by the employee’s line manager, taking into account team capacity and business requirements. Requests will be considered on a first-come, first-served basis, but priority may be given to critical business needs. Managers will aim to respond to leave requests promptly and fairly. The company may decline or request adjustments to leave dates where necessary to maintain operational continuity.

## Carry-over
Employees are encouraged to take their full annual leave entitlement within the leave year. A maximum of 5 unused days may be carried over into the following year with prior manager approval. Carried-over leave must typically be used within the first six months of the new leave year. Any additional unused leave may be forfeited unless exceptional circumstances apply or are required by law.
